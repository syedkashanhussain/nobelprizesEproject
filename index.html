About the Nobel Prize

The Nobel Prize is a set of international awards given annually in several categories such as Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences. It was established by Alfred Nobel, the inventor of dynamite.

Nobel Prizes are awarded in the fields of Physics, Chemistry, Physiology or Medicine, Literature, and Peace. In 1968, Sweden's central bank funded the Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel. These prizes are among the most prestigious awards in their fields.

Famous Nobel Laureates

Albert Einstein

Won the Nobel Prize in Physics (1921) for his discovery of the photoelectric effect.

Marie Curie

First woman to win the Nobel Prize — in both Physics (1903) and Chemistry (1911).

Malala Yousafzai

Won the Peace Prize (2014) for her struggle against the suppression of children and young people.

Nelson Mandela

Won the Nobel Peace Prize (1993) for his work in ending apartheid and fostering reconciliation.
