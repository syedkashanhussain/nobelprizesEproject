Frequently Asked Questions

The Nobel Prize is a prestigious international award given for achievements in various fields.

Alfred Nobel, a Swedish inventor, founded the Nobel Prize in his will.

Nobel Prizes are awarded annually on December 10, the anniversary of Alfred Nobel's death.

There are six Nobel Prize categories: Peace, Literature, Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, and Economic Sciences.

Different committees select winners, including the Nobel Assembly, the Swedish Academy, and the Norwegian Nobel Committee.

Since 1974, Nobel Prizes cannot be awarded posthumously unless the recipient dies after the announcement.

Prizes are awarded in Stockholm, Sweden, except for the Peace Prize which is awarded in Oslo, Norway.

The Nobel Prize includes a medal, a diploma, and a monetary award.
