Nobel Prize in Chemistry

The Nobel Prize in Chemistry is one of the six Nobel Prizes established by the will of Alfred Nobel, the inventor of dynamite.

It has been awarded annually since 1901 by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences to individuals or groups who have made outstanding contributions to the field of chemistry.

The Nobel Prize in Chemistry is one of the most prestigious scientific awards, given annually to individuals or groups who have made outstanding contributions to the field of chemistry.

The Nobel Prize in Chemistry continues to inspire innovation and excellence, pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the molecular sciences.

Nobel Prize in Chemistry

  • Established:

    The award was first given in 1901.

  • Total Awards:

    As of 2024, the Nobel Prize in Chemistry has been awarded 116 times to 195 individuals.

  • Double Laureates:

    Frederick Sanger and Barry Sharpless are notable recipients who have won the Chemistry Prize twice.

  • Prize Money:

    In 2024, the prize money was set at 11 million Swedish kronor.

How is the Nobel Prize in Chemistry Awarded?

  1. Nominations

    EApproximately 3,000 experts worldwide are invited to submit nominations each year.

  2. Review

    Nominations are thoroughly reviewed by committees, and the final decision is made by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.

  3. Decision

    The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences makes the final decision

  4. Announcement

    Winners are announced in October, and the award is presented on December 10 in Stockholm.
