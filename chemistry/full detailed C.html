1961 Melvin Calvin USA For his research on the mechanism of photosynthesis.
1962 Max Perutz, John Kendrew United Kingdom For their work on the structure of hemoglobin and myoglobin.

1965 Roderick M. Macfarlane USA For his work in physical chemistry.
1966 Robert W. Holley USA For his work on the structure of the RNA molecule.
1967 George Wald USA For his discoveries in the field of biochemistry, particularly with respect to vision.
1968 Robert W. Holley, Har Gobind Khorana, Marshall W. Nirenberg USA For their work on the structure of RNA and its relationship to protein synthesis.
1969 Peter D. Mitchell United Kingdom For his contributions to the understanding of the electron transport chain and its role in cellular respiration.
1970 Sir Bernard L. Feringa Netherlands For his work on the molecular machines of the cell.

1972 John B. Goodenough USA For his work on the development of rechargeable lithium-ion batteries.
1973 Peter D. Mitchell United Kingdom For his research in bioenergetics, especially in the area of ATP synthesis.
1974 George P. Smith, Sir Richard R. Dawkins USA For their groundbreaking research in molecular biology.
1975 Howard Temin, David Baltimore USA For their discovery of reverse transcription in retroviruses.
1976 Stanley B. Prusiner USA For his discovery of prions, a new class of infectious proteins.
1977 Paul D. Boyer, John E. Walker USA, United Kingdom For their work on the ATP synthase enzyme and the mechanism of ATP production in the cell.
1978 Albert A. Michelson USA For his precision optical instruments and their contributions to the study of light.
1979 Herbert C. Brown USA For his work on organoboranes and their applications in organic synthesis.
1980 Paul Berg, Walter Gilbert, Frederick Sanger USA, United Kingdom For their discoveries in molecular biology, particularly the development of DNA sequencing techniques.
1981 Kenichi Fukui, Roald Hoffmann Japan, USA For their theories, based on the concept of reaction paths, on the course of chemical reactions.
1982 Aaron Klug United Kingdom For his work on the structure of biological macromolecules.
1983 Henry Taube Canada/USA For his work on the mechanisms of electron transfer reactions, particularly in metal complexes.
1984 Robert F. Curl Jr., Sir Harold W. Kroto, Richard E. Smalley USA, United Kingdom For their discovery of fullerenes.
1985 Herbert A. Hauptman, Jerome Karle USA For their work on the methods of determining the structures of molecules through X-ray crystallography.
1986 Charles J. Pedersen, Donald J. Cram USA For their work on the development of molecules that could interact specifically with other molecules (molecular machines).
1987 Jean-Marie Gustave Le Clézio France For his work on the synthesis of organic compounds in biological processes.
1988 Johann Deisenhofer, Robert Huber, Hartmut Michel Germany For their work on the structure of proteins involved in photosynthesis.
1989 Sidney Altman, Thomas Cech USA For their work on the catalytic properties of RNA.
1990 Elias James Corey USA For his development of the theory of organic synthesis.
1991 Richard R. Schrock, Robert H. Grubbs, Yves Chauvin USA, France For their work on the development of metathesis reactions in organic chemistry.
1992 Rudolph A. Marcus Canada/USA For his work on the theory of electron transfer reactions in chemical systems.
1993 Kary B. Mullis, Michael Smith USA, Canada For their contributions to the development of techniques in molecular biology, including PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) and site-directed mutagenesis.
1994 George P. Smith, Sir Richard Roberts, Elizabeth H. Blackburn USA, UK, USA For their work on the discovery of the structure of telomeres and its role in cell biology.
1995 Paul D. Boyer, John E. Walker, Jens C. Skou USA, UK, Denmark For their discoveries concerning the mechanism of ATP synthesis in the cell.
1996 Robert F. Furchgott, Louis J. Ignarro, Ferid Murad USA For their discoveries concerning the signaling role of nitric oxide in the cardiovascular system.
1997 Paul D. Boyer, John E. Walker, Jens C. Skou USA, UK, Denmark For their work on the enzymatic synthesis of ATP and its significance in cell metabolism.
1998 Ahmed Zewail USA For his work on femtochemistry and the study of chemical reactions on the femtosecond timescale.
1999 Aaron Klug United Kingdom For his work on the structure of biological macromolecules using X-ray crystallography and electron microscopy.
2000 Alan J. Heeger, Alan G. MacDiarmid, Hideki Shirakawa USA, Japan For their discoveries in conductive polymers and their applications in electronics.
2001 William S. Knowles, Ryoma Noyori, K. Barry Sharpless USA, Japan For their work on asymmetric catalysis and its application to the synthesis of enantiomerically pure molecules.
2002 John B. Fenn, Koichi Tanaka, Kurt Wüthrich USA, Japan, Switzerland For their development of methods in mass spectrometry and the analysis of biomolecules.
2003 Peter Agre, Roderick MacKinnon USA For their discoveries concerning channels in cell membranes.
2004 Aaron Ciechanover, Avram Hershko, Irwin Rose Israel, USA For their work on ubiquitin-mediated protein degradation.
2005 Yves Chauvin, Robert H. Grubbs, Richard R. Schrock France, USA For their work on the development of the metathesis method in organic synthesis.
2006 Roger D. Kornberg USA For his studies of the molecular basis of eukaryotic transcription.
2007 Gerhard Ertl Germany For his studies of chemical processes on solid surfaces.
2008 Osamu Shimomura, Martin Chalfie, Roger Y. Tsien Japan, USA For the discovery and development of the green fluorescent protein, GFP.
2009 Venkatraman Ramakrishnan, Thomas A. Steitz, Ada E. Yonath UK, USA, Israel For their studies of the structure and function of the ribosome.
2010 Richard F. Heck, Ei-ichi Negishi, Akira Suzuki USA, Japan For their work on palladium-catalyzed cross-coupling in organic synthesis.
2011 Daniel Schechtman Israel For the discovery of quasicrystals.
2012 Robert Lefkowitz, Brian Kobilka USA For their work on G-protein-coupled receptors.
2013 Martin Karplus, Michael Levitt, Arieh Warshel USA, Israel For the development of multiscale models for complex chemical systems.
2014 Eric Betzig, Stefan W. Hell, William E. Moerner USA, Germany For the development of super-resolved fluorescence microscopy.
2015 Tomas Lindahl, Paul L. Modrich, Aziz Sancar UK, USA, Turkey/USA For their mechanistic studies of DNA repair.
2016 Jean-Pierre Sauvage, Sir Fraser Stoddart, Bernard L. Feringa France, UK/USA, Netherlands For the design and synthesis of molecular machines.
2017 Jacques Dubochet, Richard Henderson, Joachim Frank Switzerland, UK, USA For developing cryo-electron microscopy for the high-resolution structure determination of biomolecules in solution.
2018 Frances H. Arnold, George Smith, Sir Gregory Winter USA, UK For the directed evolution of enzymes and phage display of peptides and antibodies.
2019 John B. Goodenough, M. Stanley Whittingham, Akira Yoshino USA, UK, Japan For the development of lithium-ion batteries.
2020 Emmanuelle Charpentier, Jennifer A. Doudna France, USA For the development of CRISPR-Cas9, a method for genome editing.
2021 Benjamin List, David W.C. MacMillan Germany, UK For the development of asymmetric organocatalysis.
2022 Carolyn R. Bertozzi, Morten Meldal, K. Barry Sharpless USA, Denmark, USA For the development of click chemistry and bioorthogonal chemistry.
2023 Moungi Bawendi, Louis Brus, Alexey Ekimov USA, Russia For the discovery and development of quantum dots.
2024 David Baker, Demis Hassabis, John Jumper USA, UK For their contributions to protein folding using AI technologies.
