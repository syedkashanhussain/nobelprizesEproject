Nobel Prize Winners (1901-2025)

Year Winner(s) Country Contribution
1901 Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen Germany For the discovery of X-rays.
1902 Hendrik Antoon Lorentz, Pieter Zeeman Netherlands For their work on the influence of magnetism upon radiation phenomena.
1903 Henri Becquerel, Marie Curie, Pierre Curie Poland/France For their joint research on radiation phenomena.
1904 John William Strutt, 3rd Baron Rayleigh United Kingdom For the discovery of argon in atmospheric air.
1905 Albert A. Michelson USA For his precision optical instruments and the spectroscopic and geometric determinations of the speed of light.
1906 J.J. Thomson United Kingdom For his theoretical and experimental investigations on the conduction of electricity by gases.
1907 Albert Michelson USA For his precision optical instruments and the spectroscopic and geometric determinations of the speed of light.
1908 Gabriel Lippmann France For his method of reproducing colors photographically based on the phenomenon of interference.
1909 Guglielmo Marconi, Ferdinand Braun Italy, Germany For their contributions to the development of wireless telegraphy.
1910 Johannes Diderik van der Waals Netherlands For his work on the equation of state for gases and liquids.
1911 Marie Curie Poland/France For her services to the advancement of chemistry by the discovery of radium and polonium.
1912 Nils Gustaf Dalén Sweden For his invention of automatic regulators for use in conjunction with gas accumulators for illuminating lighthouses and buoys.
1913 Heike Kamerlingh Onnes Netherlands For his investigations on the properties of matter at low temperatures which led, inter alia, to the liquefaction of helium.
1914 Max von Laue Germany For his discovery of X-ray diffraction in crystals.
1915 William Lawrence Bragg, William Henry Bragg United Kingdom For their services to the study of crystal structure by means of X-rays.
1917 Charles Glover Barkla United Kingdom For his discovery of the characteristic Röntgen radiation of the elements.
1918 Max Planck Germany For his origin and development of the quantum theory.
1919 Johannes Stark Germany For his discovery of the Doppler effect in canal rays and the splitting of spectral lines in electric fields.
1920 Charles Édouard Guillaume Switzerland For his precision optical instruments and the spectroscopic and geometric determinations of the speed of light.
1921 Albert Einstein Germany For his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
1922 Niels Bohr Denmark For his investigations of the structure of atoms and the radiation emanating from them.
1923 Robert Andrews Millikan USA For his work on the elementary charge of electricity and on the photoelectric effect.
1924 Manne Siegbahn Sweden For his work on X-ray spectroscopy.
1925 James Franck, Gustav Hertz Germany For their experiments regarding the inelastic scattering of electrons by atoms.
1926 Owen Richardson United Kingdom For his work on the thermionic phenomenon and especially for the discovery of the law named after him.
1927 Arthur Holly Compton, Charles Thomson Rees Wilson USA, United Kingdom For their discovery of the effect named after them.
1928 Owen Willans Richardson United Kingdom For his work on the thermionic phenomenon and especially for the discovery of the law named after him.
1929 Louis de Broglie France For his discovery of the wave nature of electrons.
1930 C.V. Raman India For his work on the scattering of light and for the discovery of the effect named after him.
1931 Carl David Anderson USA For his discovery of the positron.
1932 Werner Heisenberg Germany For the creation of quantum mechanics.
1933 Erwin Schrödinger, Paul Adrien Maurice Dirac Austria, United Kingdom For the discovery of new productive forms of atomic theory.
1934 Manne Siegbahn Sweden For his work on X-ray spectroscopy.
1935 James Chadwick United Kingdom For his discovery of the neutron.
1936 Victor Franz Hess Austria For his discovery of cosmic rays.
1937 Clinton Joseph Davisson, Lester Halbert Germer USA For their experimental discovery of the diffraction of electrons by crystals.
1938 Otto Hahn Germany For his discovery of the fission of heavy nuclei.
1939 Ernest O. Lawrence USA For the invention of the cyclotron and its application to nuclear physics.
1944 Otto Hahn Germany For his discovery of the fission of uranium and the release of nuclear energy.
1945 Wolfgang Pauli Austria For his discovery of the Exclusion Principle.
1946 Percy Williams Bridgman USA For his work on the physics of high pressure.
1947 Sir Edward Victor Appleton United Kingdom For his investigations of the ionosphere and for his discovery of the Appleton layer.
1948 Patrick Maynard Stuart Blackett United Kingdom For his work on the detection and the analysis of cosmic rays.
1949 Hideki Yukawa Japan For his prediction of the existence of mesons on the basis of theoretical work on nuclear forces.
1950 Cecil Frank Powell United Kingdom For his development of the photographic method of studying nuclear processes and his discoveries regarding mesons made with this method.
1951 John Cockcroft, Ernest Walton United Kingdom For their pioneer work on the transmutation of atomic nuclei by artificially accelerated atomic particles.
1952 Felix Bloch, Edward Mills Purcell USA For their development of new methods for nuclear magnetic precision measurements and discoveries in connection therewith.
1953 Frits Zernike Netherlands For his demonstration of the phase contrast method, especially for his invention of the phase contrast microscope.
1954 Max Born, Walther Bothe Germany For their research on quantum mechanics.
1955 Yuan T. Lee, Tsung-Dao Lee, Chen Ning Yang China, USA For their theoretical work on the law of parity violation in weak interactions.
1956 John Bardeen, William Shockley, Walter Brattain USA For their researches on semiconductors and their discovery of the transistor effect.
1957 Chen Ning Yang, Tsung-Dao Lee China, USA For their work on the law of parity violation in weak interactions.
1958 Peter Debye Netherlands For his work on the theory of molecular structure.
1959 Emilio Segrè, Owen Chamberlain USA For their discovery of the antiproton.
1960 Donald A. Glaser USA For the invention of the bubble chamber.
1961 Robert Hofstadter, Rudolf Ludwig Mössbauer USA, Germany For their researches on electron scattering and nuclear reactions.
1962 Lev Landau USSR For his theoretical work on condensed matter physics, especially the theory of superconductivity and superfluidity.
1963 Maria Goeppert Mayer, J. Hans D. Jensen USA, Germany For their discoveries in the structure of the atomic nucleus.
1964 Charles Hard Townes, Arthur Leonard Schawlow USA For their contributions to the development of laser spectroscopy.
1965 Richard P. Feynman, Julian Schwinger, Sin-Itiro Tomonaga USA, Japan For their fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics, with deep-ploughing consequences for the physics of elementary particles.
1966 Alfonso García-Ballester, Alvaro Martínez-Peña Spain For the development of the theory of classical mechanics and its modern formulation.
1967 Hans Albrecht Bethe USA For his contributions to the theory of stellar reactions.
1968 Luis Alvarez USA For his contributions to the physics of elementary particles.
1969 Murray Gell-Mann USA For his contributions to the theory of elementary particles, especially the theory of quarks.
1970 Hannes Alfvén Sweden For his contributions to plasma physics, especially the theory of magnetohydrodynamics.
1971 Dennis Gabor United Kingdom For his invention and development of the holographic method.
1972 John Bardeen, Leon Cooper, Robert Schrieffer USA For their jointly developed theory of superconductivity, usually called the BCS-theory.
1973 Leo Esaki, Ivar Giaever Japan, USA For their experimental discoveries regarding tunneling phenomena in semiconductors.
1974 Sir Martin Ryle, Antony Hewish United Kingdom For their pioneering research on radio astronomy.
1975 Richard P. Feynman, Julian Schwinger, Sin-Itiro Tomonaga USA, Japan For their contributions to quantum electrodynamics and their predictions of the behavior of elementary particles.
1976 Burton Richter, Samuel Ting USA For their independently developed discovery of a new particle, the J/psi meson, which confirmed the existence of the quark.
1977 Philip W. Anderson, John H. Van Vleck, Nevill Mott USA, UK For their theoretical investigations of condensed matter systems, particularly in the field of magnetism and the properties of materials.
1978 Arno Penzias, Robert Wilson USA For their discovery of cosmic microwave background radiation, providing evidence for the Big Bang theory of the universe.
1979 Sheldon Glashow, Abdus Salam, Steven Weinberg USA, Pakistan, USA For their contributions to the theory of the unified weak force and electromagnetic interaction between elementary particles.
1980 James Peebles, Robert Dicke USA For their contributions to the understanding of the physical properties of the universe.
1981 Nicolaas Bloembergen, Arthur Leonard Schawlow USA For their contributions to the development of laser spectroscopy and the study of atomic and molecular interactions.
1982 Kenneth G. Wilson USA For his theory of critical phenomena in connection with phase transitions.
1983 Leo Esaki, Ivar Giaever Japan, USA For their experimental discoveries regarding tunneling phenomena in semiconductors.
1984 Carleton Gajdusek, Baron J. M. L. Pattison USA For their discoveries in the field of prion diseases, also known as the theory of transmissible spongiform encephalopathies.
1985 Alva Myrdal Sweden For her work in nuclear disarmament and world peace.
1986 Gerard Mourou, Donna Strickland USA, Canada For their method of generating high-intensity, ultra-short optical pulses, a revolutionary technique in laser physics.
1987 Claude Cohen-Tannoudji, Steven Chu, William D. Phillips France, USA For their contributions to the cooling and trapping of atoms with laser light, leading to advances in quantum optics and atom manipulation.
1988 John C. Mather, George F. Smoot USA For their work with the Cosmic Background Explorer Satellite (COBE), measuring the heat radiation from the Big Bang.
1989 Norman F. Ramsey USA For his work on the development of atomic clocks and their application in precision measurements of time and frequency.
1990 James Peebles USA For his theoretical discoveries in cosmology and his role in understanding the structure of the universe.
1991 Gerard Mourou, Donna Strickland USA, Canada For their groundbreaking work in the field of high-energy physics and ultra-fast lasers.
1992 Georges Charpak France For his invention and development of particle detectors, particularly the multiwire proportional chamber.
1993 Russell A. Hulse, Joseph H. Taylor USA For their discovery of the first binary pulsar and their contributions to the experimental confirmation of general relativity.
1994 Bertram Brockhouse, Clifford G. Shull Canada, USA For their development of neutron scattering techniques to investigate the structure of materials at the atomic level.
1995 Martin L. Perl, Frederick Reines USA For their contributions to the discovery of the neutrino and the tau lepton.
1996 Robert F. Curl, Sir Harold Kroto, Richard E. Smalley USA, UK For their discovery of fullerenes, a new form of carbon molecules, including the famous "buckyballs."
1997 Steven Chu, Claude Cohen-Tannoudji, William D. Phillips USA, France For their work on cooling and trapping atoms with laser light, leading to new experimental tools in quantum physics.
1998 Robert B. Laughlin, Horst L. Stoermer, Daniel C. Tsui USA For their discovery of the fractional quantum Hall effect, which advanced the field of condensed matter physics.
1999 Gerard 't Hooft, Martinus J. G. Veltman Netherlands For their work in quantum field theory and the development of the electroweak theory in particle physics.
2000 Jack S. Kilby, Robert N. Noyce USA For their work in the invention of the integrated circuit, which revolutionized electronics and computing.
2001 Eric A. Cornell, Carl E. Wieman, Wolfgang Ketterle USA, Germany For their achievements in creating the first Bose-Einstein condensate, a new state of matter at near absolute zero temperatures.
2002 Raymond Davis Jr., Masatoshi Koshiba, Riccardo Giacconi USA, Japan For their contributions to astrophysics, particularly the detection of neutrinos from the Sun and the discovery of cosmic X-ray sources.
2003 Alexei A. Abrikosov, Vitaly L. Ginzburg, Anthony J. Leggett Russia, UK, USA For their pioneering work in condensed matter physics, especially superconductivity and superfluidity.
2004 David J. Gross, H. David Politzer, Frank Wilczek USA For the discovery of asymptotic freedom in the theory of the strong interaction, which deepened our understanding of quantum chromodynamics.
2005 Roy J. Glauber, John L. Hall, Theodor W. Hänsch USA, Germany For their contributions to the development of quantum optics and the measurement of light's interaction with matter.
2006 John C. Mather, George F. Smoot USA For their work with the Cosmic Background Explorer Satellite (COBE), measuring the heat radiation from the Big Bang.
2007 Albert A. Michelson USA For his precision optical measurements and the development of techniques in precision measurement of time and light.
2008 Yoichiro Nambu, Makoto Kobayashi, Toshihide Maskawa USA, Japan For the discovery of the mechanism of spontaneous broken symmetry in subatomic physics, and the theory of quarks and leptons.
2009 Charles M. Rice, Harold Varmus USA For their discoveries of the mechanisms in the molecular biology of the viral genome and its ability to integrate into the host cell's DNA.
2010 Andre Geim, Konstantin Novoselov UK, Russia For their groundbreaking experiments with graphene, a novel material with remarkable electrical, optical, and mechanical properties.
2011 Saul Perlmutter, Brian P. Schmidt, Adam G. Riess USA For the discovery of the accelerating expansion of the Universe through observations of distant supernovae.
2012 Serge Haroche, David J. Wineland France, USA For ground-breaking experimental methods that enable measuring and manipulation of individual quantum systems.
2013 François Englert, Peter Higgs Belgium, UK For the theoretical discovery of a mechanism that contributes to our understanding of the origin of mass of subatomic particles.
2014 Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, Shuji Nakamura Japan, USA For the invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes, which have enabled bright and energy-saving white light sources.
2015 Takaaki Kajita, Arthur B. McDonald Japan, Canada For the discovery of neutrino oscillations, which shows that neutrinos have mass.
2016 David J. Thouless, F. Duncan M. Haldane, J. Michael Kosterlitz UK, USA For theoretical discoveries of topological phase transitions and topological phases of matter.
2017 Rainer Weiss, Barry C. Barish, Kip S. Thorne Germany, USA For their decisive contributions to the LIGO detector and the observation of gravitational waves.
2018 Arthur Ashkin, Gérard Mourou, Donna Strickland USA, France, Canada For groundbreaking inventions in the field of laser physics, especially in laser trapping and optical tweezers.
2019 James Peebles, Michel Mayor, Didier Queloz Canada, Switzerland, USA For contributions to our understanding of the universe, particularly the discovery of exoplanets orbiting a solar-type star.
2020 Roger Penrose, Reinhard Genzel, Andrea Ghez UK, Germany, USA For their discoveries about black holes, including proof that black holes are a natural consequence of general relativity.
2021 Syukuro Manabe, Klaus Hasselmann, Giorgio Parisi USA, Germany, Italy For their work on complex physical systems, including contributions to climate modeling and understanding chaotic systems.
2022 Alice Ball, Gauri Maung, Arwin Soler USA For their work in the development of treatments and medications that have led to significant advancements in medical science.
2023 Pierre Agostini, Anne L'Huillier, F. Krausz , France, Germany For the discovery of the interaction of light with matter in attosecond time scales, advancing the study of electron dynamics.
2024 John J. Hopfield and Geoffrey Hinton USA and Wimbledon, London, United Kingdom For their contributions to artificial neural networks and deep learning, which have become a foundation for modern machine learning and artificial intelligence.
2025 To Be Announced To Be Announced To Be Announced
