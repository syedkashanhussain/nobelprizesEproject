About the Prize

Nobel Prize in Economics Sciences

The Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences, officially called the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel.

is awarded annually to individuals or groups who have made significant contributions to the field of economics.

While not part of Alfred Nobel's original will, it holds similar prestige and is administered in accordance with the same protocols as the original Nobel Prizes.

The Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences continues to be a cornerstone in recognizing and inspiring groundbreaking research that shapes how economies function, how policies are developed, and how global challenges are addressed.

Recent Developments in Economics Sciences

The 2024 Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences was awarded jointly to Daron Acemoglu, Simon Johnson, and James A. Robinson for their groundbreaking research on how institutions shape economic prosperity. How is the Nobel Prize inEconomics Sciences Awarded?

The Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences, officially called the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel, is awarded annually to individuals or groups for exceptional contributions to the field of economics. Below is a detailed explanation of how the prize is awarded:

  1. Nominations

    Each year, in September, the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences sends invitations to eligible nominators worldwide. These include:

    • Professors of economics and related fields.
    • Members of academies and institutions in economics or social sciences.
    • Nobel laureates in economics from previous years.
    • Other qualified experts selected by the Nobel committees.

  2. Award Ceremony

    The prize is awarded on December 10, the anniversary of Alfred Nobel’s death.

    The ceremony is held in Stockholm, alongside the other Nobel Prizes (except the Peace Prize, awarded in Oslo).

    The laureate receives:

    • A gold medal
    • A diploma
    • A monetary award which varies each year (approximately 10 million SEK in recent years).

  3. Announcement

    The winner(s) are announced in October during a press conference held by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences in Stockholm.

    The laureate(s) are informed just before the public announcement.
